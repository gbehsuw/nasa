Making a Paper Windmill

Purpose

Our goal is to create a simple version of a windmill in real life!


Like a real windmill the stronger and clearer the wind blowing on the windmill is, the more it’ll spin! Just like the potential of wind energy in real life, locations where there is open space allow for a higher potential of clear and strong wind energy without structures blocking the wind. Once finished, try putting the windmill outside your house instead of inside, this is similar to how putting wind turbines offshore allows for more clear wind to power the turbines.

Materials

To make this you will need:

  • Paper (Preferably square and about 15cm x 15cm)
  • Scrissors
  • Starw/Pencil (w/ eraser)/Skewer
  • Thumbtack/Push Pin

1. Prepare the Paper

Start with a square piece of paper. If you don’t have a square, you can cut a regular piece of paper into a square by folding one corner diagonally and cutting off the excess.

2. Draw Lines

Use a ruler to draw two diagonal lines from corner to corner, crossing at the center of the square. You should now have an "X" shape on the paper.

3. Cut Along the Lines

Cut along the lines you drew, stopping about 1 inch from the center (don't cut all the way to the center, just leave a small space).

4. Fold the Corners

Take every other corner of the paper and gently fold it toward the center of the square. You’ll be folding four of the corners in total.

5. Secure the Windmill

Push a push pin or thumbtack through the four folded corners and the center of the paper. Then, press the pin into the eraser of a pencil or use a straw or skewer for the base, making sure the windmill can spin freely.
